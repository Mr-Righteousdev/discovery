Kenya Safaris

Kenya - The Birthplace of Safari

Kenya offers the quintessential African safari experience. From the vast plains of the Maasai Mara where the Great Migration unfolds, to the majestic elephants of Amboseli with Kilimanjaro as a backdrop, Kenya delivers unforgettable wildlife encounters. Experience the rich Maasai culture, explore the Great Rift Valley lakes, and discover why this is where safari began.

Kenya Highlights

Maasai Mara

Witness the Great Migration, Big Five sightings, and authentic Maasai culture.

Amboseli

Elephant herds with the stunning backdrop of Mount Kilimanjaro.

Tsavo National Park

Red-dust elephants, maneless lions, and vast wilderness landscapes.

Lake Nakuru

Pink flamingos, rhinos, and baboon cliff viewpoints.

Best Time to Visit Kenya

June to October: Dry season, best for wildlife viewing. The Great Migration is typically July-September in the Mara.

January to March: Short dry season with excellent bird watching and fewer crowds.

Practical Information

Visa

Most nationalities can obtain eVisa online or on arrival.

Currency

Kenyan Shilling (KES). USD widely accepted.

Language

English and Swahili are official languages.

Climate

Tropical climate with regional variations. Daytime 20-30°C.
